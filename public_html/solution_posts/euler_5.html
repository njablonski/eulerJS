5. Smallest multiple

Read the problem definition at projecteuler.net

2520 is the smallest number that can be divided by each of the numbers from 1 to 10 without any remainder.

What is the smallest positive number that is evenly divisible by all of the numbers from 1 to 20?

Our code needs the ability to check whether a number fits the criterion: divisibility by the numbers 1-20.

We'll write a function to handle that called isDivisibleByRange(). It takes in a number, tests it for divisibility by the numbers 11-20, and returns true only if it passes. We don't need to test for divisibility by the numbers 1-10, as they are all factors of at least one number in the range 11-20 (i.e. any number divisible by 20 is also divisible by 10, 5, 4, 2, and 1).

Our main function has an incrementing loop that runs isDivisibleByRange() until a number passes. From the fundamental theorem of arithmetic we encountered in Problem 3, we know that prime factors of a number cannot be reduced or divided out of that number's unique prime factorization. Therefore, we can multiply together all of the prime numbers in the range 1-20, round that product down to the nearest multiple of 20, and start our search from the number 9699680. We'll then increment by 20 each loop until we find a number that passes.

It takes about 10 seconds for my computer to reach the correct result of 232792560 with this code.
